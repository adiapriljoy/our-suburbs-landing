Salisbury

Situated just 10 km south of the Brisbane CBD, Salisbury offers savvy buyers a compelling blend of convenience and strong growth potential. The suburb is well-connected by rail and major arterial roads, making it highly accessible for commuters. With nearby schools, parks, and shopping hubs, it strikes a balance between family vitality and value. The recent surge in demand for inner-ring suburbs has bumped Salisbury into the spotlight—capital growth is robust, and the area is increasingly appealing as both an owner-occupier and investor location.

From an investment perspective, Salisbury offers an opportunity to capture both long-term growth and rental income. The market data shows healthy annual growth for houses and consistent rental demand in the region. This makes it a smart choice for those looking to build equity and secure steady returns within Brisbane’s metropolitan footprint.

  • Median house price: $1,222,500
  • Annual growth: 11.1%
  • Rental yield (houses): 3.0%
  • Median weekly rent: $625

Coopers Plains

Coopers Plains offers a particularly strong value proposition for home‑buyers and investors seeking to strike the balance between convenience and growth.

Located within Brisbane’s inner southern corridor, the suburb benefits from excellent transport links, nearby retail and dining hubs, and a residential mix that supports both modern living and accessibility.

Whether you’re a professional seeking a manageable commute or a family looking for a well‑connected home, Coopers Plains brings you close to the action without the premium price tag of some adjacent suburbs.

From an investment standpoint, Coopers Plains is showing encouraging signs of momentum—both in capital growth and rental performance.

Moorooka

Moorooka is a vibrant and culturally rich suburb that offers exceptional value and connectivity just south of Brisbane’s CBD.

With its lively café scene, diverse restaurants and the well‑known "Magic Mile" of motor and trade outlets, Moorooka provides a dynamic lifestyle for families, professionals and investors alike.

At the same time, its established residential streets and good access to transport links mean you can enjoy inner‑city convenience without inner‑city prices.

From an investment and ownership perspective, Moorooka’s growth story is compelling. The suburb is delivering above‑average capital gains while still offering reasonable entry points compared with some of Brisbane’s more premium suburbs.

  • Median house price: $1,240,000
  • Annual growth: 7.8%
  • Rental yield (houses): 2.7%
  • Median weekly rent: $640

Greater Brisbane

Brisbane's property market is in an extraordinary boom, driven by record interstate migration and a critical lack of housing supply. This vigorous growth has fundamentally reset the city's position in the national landscape. No longer the affordable alternative, Brisbane is now experiencing rapid value appreciation across all dwelling types, reflecting its increasing appeal as a major Australian capital.

A key measure of this market shift is Brisbane now holding Australia's second-highest median house price, consistently surpassing $1 million. Intense competition for limited stock is forcing demand and price growth across both inner and outer suburbs. This premium status is further cemented by the unit market, where median values are also ranking second nationally, reinforcing the structural re-rating of Brisbane property.

The market's future remains positive, underpinned by population inflows and significant infrastructure spending, especially for the 2032 Olympics. However, the sheer pace of growth is expected to slow due to affordability limits. Persistent low inventory and high construction costs continue to exacerbate supply shortages, ensuring the market remains tight and competitive despite any moderation.
